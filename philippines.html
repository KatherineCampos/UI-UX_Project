PHILIPPINES

The Philippines is located in Southeast Asia with 7,000 islands and they are divided into three main geographic regions, Luzon to be the largest and where the Manila, the capital city is located. Second largest region is Mindanao commonly known as Southern Philippines and finally the smallest region and is the central region of the Philippines. Philippines weather is tropical with two seasons, summer and rain.

Explore this tropical country by enjoying the white sand beaches like Polompon Island in Leyte and Palawan, try the mouth-watering exotic food or the freshly cook seafood, stroll around the historical places like Batanes and Leyte.


Batanes

            Discover this smallest province in the Philippines, Batanes. This is one of the historical places in the Philippines. If you are a person who loves exploring other countries culture, visit this place and relish with their exquisite culture. And, dive into their beautiful beaches and lagoons, climb a mountain and discover the beauty of their caves, or just simply relax on one of their small islands.

Leyte

            Leyte is a province located in Visayas Region. It has two major cities, Ormoc and the port of Tacloban. Leyte is one of the historical places in the Philippines because it is the place where Gen. Douglas McArthur and his team landed to help our forefathers to fight against the Japanese invasion during the World War II. So, schedule a visit with this place and discover its historic story. Aside from that, enjoy riding a habal-habal, a motorbike with a wide seat and a roof, while doing your site seeing. Stop by one local street stand and try their special street foods. You can also hire a local boat and go on a river cruise in Sohoton Natural Bridge Park where you can find a cathedral style underground cave.

Palawan

            Palawan is a pristine island located eastern Visayas Region. Palawan is rich with natural wonders. So, add Palawan on your bucket list and swim through underwater mountains, snorkel over reefs, visit the amazingly small and big lagoons, tour on their astonishing waterfalls, and feel the air as you slide down the 750-meter-long zip lines.
